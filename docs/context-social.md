# MealMesh — Social Content Context

> Feed this file to any LLM along with a platform + pillar to draft a social
> post. It defines the value props, the five content pillars, per-platform
> format rules, a hook/CTA bank, and the do/don't list that keeps social
> content as honest as the rest of the brand.

---

## 1. One-liner + value props

**One-liner:** "One household. Many diets. One plan. One grocery list."

**Value props to rotate through** (pick one per post, don't cram all of
them in):
- One person halal, one gluten-free, one picky — one plan that works for
  everyone, not five separate dinners.
- A positive allow-list checked ingredient-by-ingredient, not just a list of
  "don'ts" handed to an AI and hoped for the best.
- One consolidated grocery list for the whole household, every week.
- Regional pricing that knows what groceries actually cost where you live.
- 40+ diets and cultural/medical constraints supported — halal, kosher,
  Hindu vegetarian, diabetic, low-FODMAP, allergies, and more, at the same
  table.

## 2. Content pillars

1. **Relatable problem** — the real friction of cooking for a mixed-diet
   household: the mental load, the "what do I even make tonight" moment, the
   3-grocery-lists problem. No product mention required in the hook.
2. **Feature spotlight** — one mechanism explained simply: the allow-list,
   the deterministic safety check, regional price calibration, the grocery
   list consolidation, "cook from what I have" mode. Pick ONE per post.
3. **Real testimonial** — **only ever a real, permissioned user quote.**
   MealMesh does not fabricate testimonials, reviews, or quotes — ever. Until
   real customer words exist, **skip this pillar entirely** or substitute a
   "who this is for" persona post instead (see the sample in §5 — it
   describes a *type* of household, not an invented person saying things).
4. **Behind-the-scenes** — why a product decision was made: why a
   deterministic check matters more than a smarter AI prompt, why regional
   pricing exists, why the household model came before anything else got
   built. Builds trust through transparency, not polish.
5. **Educational** — genuinely useful diet/nutrition/cultural-food knowledge
   that stands on its own, e.g. "halal vs. kosher: what actually overlaps,"
   "what low-FODMAP actually restricts." Soft-touch product mention at most.

## 3. Per-platform guidance

| Platform | Format | Length | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Instagram** | Single image/carousel + caption, or Reel | Caption: 1-3 short paragraphs; first line is the hook (gets cut off in feed) | Carousels work well for "feature spotlight" (one mechanism per slide) and "educational" (one fact per slide). 3-5 hashtags, specific not generic (`#halalmealplanning` not `#food`). |
| **TikTok** | Short vertical video, 15-45s | Script: hook in first 2 seconds, one idea, one CTA | Best for "relatable problem" (a quick relatable moment) and "behind-the-scenes." Talk like a person, not an ad. On-screen text for the hook line. |
| **Facebook** | Text post or link share, often longer-form | 2-4 short paragraphs | Skews toward parents/older audience — "relatable problem" and "educational" pillars land best. Fine to be slightly longer/more explanatory than Instagram. |
| **LinkedIn** | Text post, founder voice | 3-6 short paragraphs, one idea per paragraph | "Behind-the-scenes" is the natural home here — building-in-public, product decisions, the moat. Avoid hard-sell CTAs; this audience responds to substance. |
| **Pinterest** | Pin image + description, links out | Description: 100-200 chars, keyword-forward (this is a search engine) | "Educational" and "feature spotlight" content repurposed as a static graphic (a checklist, a comparison). Pin description should read like a search query a user would type. |
| **X** | Single post or short thread | Single post ≤280 chars; thread: 3-6 posts | "Behind-the-scenes" and "educational" work well as threads (one idea per post). Plain, direct, no hashtag stuffing — 0-1 hashtag max. |

*(MealMesh also has a YouTube presence — treat long-form video the same as
the TikTok pillar guidance, just longer and less cut-for-time.)*

## 4. Hooks + CTA bank

**Hooks** (problem-first, no product mention):
- "If you live with other people, there's a good chance not everyone at your
  table eats the same way."
- "Halal and gluten-free sound unrelated. They're not — here's where they
  actually overlap."
- "Three grocery lists for one household dinner is how budgets quietly
  blow up."
- "Most meal apps assume one eater. Real households aren't built that way."

**CTAs** (rotate, keep them low-pressure):
- "See what one week of shared cooking looks like → [link]"
- "Build your household's plan, free → [link]"
- "One plan, every diet at your table → [link]"
- "Read the full breakdown → [link to article]"

## 6. Do / don't

**Do:**
- Use real product mechanisms (allow-list, deterministic check, regional
  pricing) as the substance of "feature spotlight" posts.
- Keep "behind-the-scenes" posts about real decisions and real tradeoffs.
- Link out to the blog for anything that needs more than a post's worth of
  explanation.
- Vary the pillar — don't post five "feature spotlight" posts in a row.

**Don't:**
- **Never fabricate a testimonial, review, quote, or "X% of users say"
  statistic.** If real testimonials don't exist yet, skip that pillar.
- **Never make an unsafe or unqualified dietary/medical claim** ("X is safe
  for diabetics," "Y is halal") — describe categories, not blanket
  guarantees; MealMesh is not a medical or religious authority (see
  `src/app/terms.tsx`).
- Don't use fake urgency or countdown framing — this is a planning tool, not
  a flash sale.
- Don't post a feature claim that's ahead of what's actually shipped (check
  `docs/MealMesh-context.md` for current state before promising a feature).
